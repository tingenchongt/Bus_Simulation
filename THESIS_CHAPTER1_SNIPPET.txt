GENERAL OBJECTIVE
-----------------
To develop and apply a mesoscopic simulation model of bus operations along the
Robinsons Pala-Pala to Waltermart Dasmariñas corridor using UXsim in Python,
calibrated from structured field observations (Data_Collection.xlsx), and to
compare corridor-level performance under baseline versus optimized stop-use
scenarios.

SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES
-------------------
1. To encode observation data from Morning Session, Lunch, and Afternoon Session
   sheets into session-specific calibration inputs, including directional trip
   counts and dwell statistics, stratified by survey Location (Robinsons vs
   Waltermart as the observation origin).

2. To implement a directed UXsim network for the study corridor with terminal
   stops, a signalized junction, a formal post-intersection bus stop, and—under
   baseline scenarios—an informal curbside segment on the Robinsons-to-
   Waltermart approach before the signal.

3. To execute twelve simulation scenarios (four per observation period) that
   pair baseline and optimized configurations under Robinsons-location and
   Waltermart-location calibration streams, and to export results to CSV files
   and network and summary figures for analysis.

4. To interpret simulator-reported indicators (e.g., average travel time,
   average delay, delay ratio, average speed, completed trips) and to derive
   policy-oriented recommendations subject to the model's stated limitations.

SCOPE (SIMULATION SLICE — align with what the code does)
--------------------------------------------------------
The UXsim portion of this study covers public bus trips represented as time-
varying origin–destination demand between Robinsons and Waltermart terminals
on a schematic corridor, with mesoscopic link dynamics and a simplified two-
phase signal. It does not, in the implemented repository, train machine learning
models for arrival prediction, compute MAE/RMSE for ETA, or optimize dispatch
headways; those may be treated as separate future extensions unless explicitly
implemented and documented elsewhere.

DELIMITATIONS (ONE SHORT PARAGRAPH YOU CAN PASTE)
-------------------------------------------------
The mesoscopic model abstracts intersection geometry and mixed traffic
(jeepneys, tricycles, etc.); passenger boarding, crowding, and traffic condition
columns from the spreadsheet are summarized for documentation but do not
currently drive endogenous crowding or variable dwell inside UXsim. Conclusions
from simulation apply to the stated scenario and should be validated against
independent field timing or GPS where possible.
